# Own your part: "I" versus "we"

Interviewers need to know what *you* did, not what happened around you. When an answer
lives in "we", the interviewer can't tell which decisions were yours, so they can't
credit you for them. The fix is not to erase the team — it's to make your own thread
visible inside the team's work.

## Diagnose the blur

Listen for these tells in your own answers:

- Every verb is plural: "we decided", "we built", "we shipped". A whole answer with no
  "I" means your specific contribution is invisible.
- Passive constructions that hide the actor: "it was decided that…", "the migration was
  done". Decided by whom? Done by whom?
- Credit that floats to the team for the wins and to no one for the calls.

## Reframe without stealing credit

Keep the team where the team belongs, and step forward on your own actions:

- Team → "Our team was migrating off the monolith."
- You → "I owned the auth service's cutover. I chose to run old and new in parallel for a
  week so we could roll back instantly, and I wrote the reconciliation checks that gave us
  confidence to flip the flag."

Notice the structure: one sentence of shared context, then first-person ownership of the
decisions and the work. You can be generous about collaboration — "I paired with our SRE
on the rollout plan" — while still owning your call.

## The drill

Take an answer that's mostly "we". Underline every plural verb. For each one, ask: what
did *I* specifically do or decide here? Rewrite at least the pivotal moments in the first
person, leaving "we" only where the work was genuinely shared.
